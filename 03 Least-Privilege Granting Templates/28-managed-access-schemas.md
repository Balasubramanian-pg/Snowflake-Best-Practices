# 28-managed-access-schemas

What is 28-managed-access-schemas and why does it matter?  
28-managed-access-schemas refers to a standardized framework for managing access to sensitive resources, ensuring that only authorized individuals or systems can access, modify, or delete critical data. This framework matters because it provides a structured approach to access management, reducing the risk of data breaches and unauthorized access.

## The Problem It Solves
What specific problem or tension led to the need for 28-managed-access-schemas?  
The problem of unauthorized access to sensitive resources, which can lead to data breaches, financial losses, and reputational damage, prompted the need for a standardized access management framework. This problem arises from the lack of a structured approach to access control, resulting in inconsistent and ineffective access management practices.

## How to Think About It
Describe the mental model for understanding this topic.  
To understand 28-managed-access-schemas, think of it as a layered framework that connects identity management, access control, and resource protection. It behaves like a hierarchical system, where access is granted or denied based on a user's identity, role, and permissions. This mental model reframes the problem of access management by providing a structured approach to managing access to sensitive resources.

## Core Truths
List the essential principles that always apply.

- **Least Privilege**: Access should be granted based on the principle of least privilege, where users are given only the necessary permissions to perform their tasks.
- **Separation of Duties**: Access control should be designed to separate duties and responsibilities, preventing a single user from having excessive control over sensitive resources.
- **Audit and Monitoring**: Access management should include regular audits and monitoring to detect and respond to potential security incidents.

## What It Looks Like in Practice
Briefly explain how 28-managed-access-schemas is typically used in real situations.  
In practice, 28-managed-access-schemas is used to manage access to sensitive resources, such as financial data, personal identifiable information, or confidential business information. The framework is typically implemented using a combination of technical controls, such as access control lists, group policy objects, and identity management systems.

## Where People Go Wrong
Common misunderstandings or misuses.

- **Overly Permissive Access**: Granting excessive access permissions to users, which can lead to unauthorized access and data breaches.
- **Inadequate Monitoring**: Failing to regularly monitor and audit access to sensitive resources, which can result in undetected security incidents.

## When to Use It and When Not To
Define clear boundaries.

**Use it when**
- **Sensitive Resources**: Access to sensitive resources, such as financial data or personal identifiable information, needs to be managed and controlled.
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Regulatory requirements, such as GDPR or HIPAA, mandate the implementation of access management controls.

**Avoid it when**
- **Publicly Available Resources**: Access to publicly available resources, such as public websites or open-source software, does not require managed access controls.
- **Low-Risk Resources**: Access to low-risk resources, such as internal blogs or company newsletters, may not require the full implementation of 28-managed-access-schemas.
