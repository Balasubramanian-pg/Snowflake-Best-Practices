# Why Small Tables Do Not Need Clustering Keys in Snowflake

**Description:**
This topic explains the concept of clustering keys in Snowflake and why they are not necessary for small tables. Clustering keys are used to optimize query performance by physically reordering the data in a table. However, for small tables, the benefits of clustering keys are often outweighed by the overhead of maintenance.

## Short Explanation
**Overview:** Clustering keys are a performance optimization feature in Snowflake that reorders data in a table to improve query performance.

**Problem Solved:** Clustering keys solve the problem of slow query performance by reordering data to match query patterns.

**Importance:** Clustering keys are important for large tables with complex query patterns, but may not be necessary for small tables.

**Use Cases:**
- Optimizing query performance for large fact tables
- Improving data loading performance for small dimension tables

### Typical Scenario
A small table with only a few thousand rows is being queried frequently. In this case, creating a clustering key may not provide significant performance benefits and may even increase maintenance overhead.
