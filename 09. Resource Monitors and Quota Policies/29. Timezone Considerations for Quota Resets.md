# Timezone Considerations for Quota Resets in Snowflake

**Description:**
This topic discusses the importance of considering timezones when resetting quotas in Snowflake. Quota resets are often scheduled to occur at specific times of the day, and timezone differences can impact when these resets occur. Understanding how Snowflake handles timezones is crucial for effective quota management.

## Short Explanation
**Overview:** Snowflake uses UTC as its default timezone, but quota resets can be affected by the timezone of the user or the warehouse.

**Problem Solved:** Ensures that quota resets occur at the correct time, taking into account timezone differences.

**Importance:** Prevents unexpected quota resets or delays, which can impact data loading and processing.

**Use Cases:**
- Resetting quotas for data loading
- Scheduling quota resets for warehouse maintenance

### Typical Scenario
A company has a Snowflake warehouse that is used for data loading and processing. The company wants to reset the quota for the warehouse at 8am PST every day. However, the Snowflake account is set to UTC timezone. To ensure that the quota reset occurs at the correct time, the company needs to consider the timezone difference between UTC and PST.

Snowflake uses the following functions to handle timezones:
- `TIMESTAMP_TZ`: converts a timestamp to a specific timezone
- `CONVERT_TIMEZONE`: converts a timestamp from one timezone to another

For example, to convert 8am PST to UTC:
```sql
SELECT CONVERT_TIMEZONE('UTC', 'America/Los_Angeles', '08:00:00');
```
