# Optimizing Serverless Task Costs with Compute Multiplier in Snowflake

**Description:**
This topic explains how to optimize serverless task costs in Snowflake by understanding the compute multiplier. Serverless tasks allow for scalable compute resources without provisioning or managing servers. However, cost optimization is crucial to ensure efficient use of resources.

**Overview:** Serverless tasks in Snowflake enable on-demand compute resources for tasks, but costs can escalate if not managed properly.

**Problem Solved:** The compute multiplier concept helps optimize serverless task costs by allowing users to control the amount of compute resources allocated.

**Importance:** Understanding the compute multiplier is crucial for cost-effective use of serverless tasks in Snowflake, especially for large-scale data processing.

A company uses serverless tasks in Snowflake to perform daily data integration from various sources. By adjusting the compute multiplier, they can balance processing speed and costs.

### Example Execution Logic Step-by-Step
The compute multiplier determines the amount of compute resources allocated to a serverless task. A higher multiplier increases processing speed but also costs. For example, a multiplier of 2 allocates twice the compute resources of a multiplier of 1.

### Implementation Example
To optimize costs, create a task with a variable compute multiplier based on the workload. For instance:
CREATE TASK my_task
  WAREHOUSE = 'my_warehouse'
  SCHEDULE = '0 8 * * *'
  COMMENT = 'Daily data integration task'
  COMPUTE_MULTIPLIER = 1.5
AS
  CALL my_procedure();
