# 04-temporary-user-access-policy

What is 04-temporary-user-access-policy and why does it matter?  
The 04-temporary-user-access-policy refers to a set of guidelines and procedures for granting temporary access to systems, data, or resources to users who require it for a limited time, ensuring the security and integrity of the organization's assets. This policy matters because it helps mitigate the risks associated with permanent access and reduces the attack surface. It is essential for maintaining compliance with regulatory requirements and industry standards.

## The Problem It Solves
What specific problem or tension led to the need for 04-temporary-user-access-policy?  
The need for temporary user access policy arose from the tension between granting users the necessary access to perform their jobs and minimizing the risk of unauthorized access, data breaches, or other security incidents. This tension is particularly pronounced in situations where contractors, vendors, or temporary employees require access to sensitive systems or data, but do not need permanent access.

## How to Think About It
Describe the mental model for understanding this topic.  
To understand the 04-temporary-user-access-policy, it is essential to think about it as a risk management strategy that balances the need for access with the need for security. This policy behaves like a temporary bridge, connecting users to the resources they need for a limited time, while reframing the problem of permanent access as a potential security risk. It connects ideas such as least privilege, segregation of duties, and access control, to create a comprehensive framework for managing temporary access.

## Core Truths
List the essential principles that always apply.

- **Least Privilege**: Grant users only the necessary access and permissions to perform their tasks.
- **Time-Limited Access**: Limit the duration of access to the minimum required to complete the task.
- **Monitoring and Review**: Regularly monitor and review temporary access to ensure it is still necessary and has not been compromised.

## What It Looks Like in Practice
Briefly explain how 04-temporary-user-access-policy is typically used in real situations.  
In practice, the 04-temporary-user-access-policy is typically used to grant temporary access to systems, data, or resources for specific projects, contracts, or tasks. The policy is structured around a clear set of procedures for requesting, approving, and revoking temporary access, with a focus on ensuring that access is granted only for the necessary duration and with the necessary permissions.

## Where People Go Wrong
Common misunderstandings or misuses.

- **Overly Broad Access**: Granting users overly broad access or permissions, which can increase the risk of unauthorized access or data breaches.
- **Inadequate Monitoring**: Failing to regularly monitor and review temporary access, which can lead to unused or compromised access going undetected.

## When to Use It and When Not To
Define clear boundaries.

**Use it when**
- **Contractors or Vendors Require Access**: Temporary access is necessary for contractors or vendors to complete a specific project or task.
- **Temporary Employees**: Temporary employees require access to systems or data to perform their job functions.

**Avoid it when**
- **Permanent Access is Required**: Users require permanent access to systems or data to perform their job functions.
- **Access is Not Necessary**: Access to systems or data is not necessary for the user to perform their job functions.

## One Line to Remember
A single sentence that captures the essence of 04-temporary-user-access-policy.
The 04-temporary-user-access-policy is a risk management strategy that grants temporary access to users who require it for a limited time, while minimizing the risk of unauthorized access or security incidents.
