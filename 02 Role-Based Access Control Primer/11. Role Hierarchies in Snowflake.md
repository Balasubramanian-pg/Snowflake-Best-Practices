# 11. Role Hierarchies in Snowflake

Role hierarchies in Snowflake allow administrators to build an inheritance structure where higher-level roles automatically inherit the privileges granted to lower-level roles.
This design minimizes administrative overhead by ensuring users assigned to senior roles seamlessly access resources permitted to their subordinate roles without requiring redundant privilege grants.

# Key Points

* **Privilege Inheritance:** When Role A is granted to Role B, Role B inherits all privileges explicitly granted to Role A, as well as any privileges Role A inherited from other roles.
* **System-Defined Roles:** Snowflake provides a default hierarchy where `ACCOUNTADMIN` inherits from `SYSADMIN` and `SECURITYADMIN`, which must be respected when designing custom architectures.
* **Custom Role Alignment:** Best practice dictates creating a hierarchy separating access roles (which hold specific object privileges) from functional roles (which map directly to business functions or users).
* **SYSADMIN Integration:** All top-level custom roles should ultimately be granted to the `SYSADMIN` role to ensure centralized management of all custom objects by account administrators.
* **Principle of Least Privilege:** Hierarchies should be designed from the bottom up, granting granular permissions to base roles and aggregating them into broader functional roles.

> [!TIP]
> Always use the `USERADMIN` role to create new roles and the `SECURITYADMIN` role to manage grants, adhering to Snowflake's built-in separation of administrative duties.

# Implementation

Provide a realistic Snowflake SQL example demonstrating the concept.

```sql
USE ROLE USERADMIN;
CREATE ROLE hr_data_read_ar;
CREATE ROLE hr_analyst_fr;

USE ROLE SECURITYADMIN;
GRANT USAGE ON DATABASE hr_db TO ROLE hr_data_read_ar;
GRANT USAGE ON SCHEMA hr_db.public TO ROLE hr_data_read_ar;
GRANT SELECT ON ALL TABLES IN SCHEMA hr_db.public TO ROLE hr_data_read_ar;

GRANT ROLE hr_data_read_ar TO ROLE hr_analyst_fr;

GRANT ROLE hr_analyst_fr TO ROLE SYSADMIN;
GRANT ROLE hr_analyst_fr TO USER jsmith;
```

# What does the Query do?

Explain the SQL step by step:

Step 1 switches to the `USERADMIN` role to create two custom roles: an access role (`hr_data_read_ar`) for database permissions and a functional role (`hr_analyst_fr`) representing a job function.

Step 2 switches to the `SECURITYADMIN` role to assign specific read-only database, schema, and table privileges directly to the access role.

Step 3 grants the access role to the functional role, establishing the hierarchy where the analyst role automatically inherits all read privileges without needing direct table grants.

Step 4 grants the functional role to the system-defined `SYSADMIN` role to ensure administrators can manage the objects, and finally assigns the functional role to a specific user (`jsmith`).

# When Should We Use This? 

* When designing an enterprise-grade security model using Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) to map database permissions to specific business functions or departments.
* When scaling Snowflake usage across multiple teams, ensuring that administrators (`SYSADMIN`) maintain visibility and control over all custom objects without manually tracking individual user grants.
